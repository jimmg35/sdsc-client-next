## The short version of what's going on in SDSC


Over the last three months, SDSC has kept leaning into one of its clearest strengths: pairing ambitious social science questions with the data and computing infrastructure needed to answer them at national scale.

[anchor:fsu-aws-research-acceleration-fund-american-electorate]
One of the clearest developments in this window is the **2025-2026 FSU/AWS Research Acceleration Fund award** to Mehak Sachdeva, A. Stewart Fotheringham, and Mason Mathews. That support expands SDSC's cloud computing capacity for large-scale spatial analysis and machine learning, especially for research examining how residential mobility and neighborhood context shape the American electorate.

[anchor:mehak-sachdeva-fyap-grant-urban-polarization]
This same stretch also includes **Mehak Sachdeva's 2025-2026 First Year Assistant Professor Grant**, which supports the construction of a high-resolution national voter database for analyzing urban polarization. The award sharpens SDSC's role in building data infrastructure that can support long-horizon research on political engagement and partisan change across the United States.
